## 1. Project Overview

**RRFA** = Representation Rerouting for Agentic Safety. We train LoRA circuit breaker (CB) adapters on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct to defend against indirect prompt injection in tool-calling agents. The CB approach pushes "harmful" hidden-state representations away from the frozen base model at specific layers (10, 20), while anchoring "benign" representations close.

**Target venue**: COLM 2026 (deadline approaching).

**Key connection**: This work extends the original Circuit Breakers paper (Zou et al. 2024) to agentic tool-calling, and connects to Jin's SRMU paper (Selective Representation Misdirection for Unlearning) via the importance-weighted rerouting concept.

### What the Paper SHOULD Become

**Core narrative**: Standard CB rerouting fails on tool-calling agents because harmful and benign tool calls share representation space (high entanglement). We characterize this via probing and identify a fundamental **selectivity problem**: the push direction is strong (AUC 0.96) but the model's ability to decide WHEN to push is weak (AUC 0.83). We propose feature-selective rerouting (inspired by SRMU) and systematic training fixes to achieve practical attack reduction.

## 4. Current Training Configuration

### Loss Function
- **Mode**: `per_token_cb` (not triplet — the paper's triplet loss was abandoned in Sweep 7)
- **Distance**: `dl2rc` = L2_norm(a-b) + 10 * ReLU(1 - cos_sim(a,b))
- **Margin**: harmful=20.0, benign=3.0 (v2 configs)
- **Hinge**: ReLU(margin - distance) — zero gradient once distance exceeds margin
- **Total**: `loss = alpha_benign * benign_loss + beta_harmful * harmful_loss + gamma_kl * kl_loss`
- **Coefficients**: alpha_b=0.5, beta_h=0.4, gamma_kl=0.3

### Schedule (v2 configs)
- **cs** (harmful push): α_max * max(0, 1 - t/(decay_mult * T)), with decay_mult=2.0
- **cr** (benign retain): α_max * min(1, t/(decay_mult * T))
- **KL**: γ_kl_eff = (cr / α_max) * γ_kl (scheduled with retain, not constant)
- **LR**: constant after warmup (not linear decay)
- **α_max**: 10.0

### Model
- Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct
- LoRA: ALL 32 layers, r=16, alpha=32, dropout=0.05
- CB target layers: 10, 20
- Training: batch_size=4, grad_accum=2, effective_batch=8
- LR: 5e-5, warmup: 20 steps

### Policy
- Training policy: `cb_full_sequence` (loss on all tokens)
- Pooling policy: `cb_full_sequence`
- Eval mode: `next_tool_prediction` (truncate to decision point, generate next tool call)

## 6. Four Training Inefficiencies Found (Fixed in v2)

1. **alpha_decay_multiplier=1.0** → cs=0 at training end. Fix: 2.0
2. **margin_harmful=1.6** → saturated in ~10-50 steps on dim-4096. Fix: 20.0
3. **KL constant at 0.3** while harmful push decays → fights the push. Fix: schedule with cr
4. **Linear LR decay** + alpha decay = (1-t)² quadratic. Fix: constant LR after warmup

| Pooling | Probe | L5 | L10 | L15 | L20 | L25 | L30 | All |
|---------|-------|-----|------|------|------|------|------|------|

**Key finding**: Pattern FLIPS from Test 1. Last_token is now BEST (0.96), mean is weakest (0.76 MLP). Same context → mean dilutes, last_token captures response difference. Best layer: 10 (0.967 MLP).

| Layer | AUC | Layer | AUC | Layer | AUC | Layer | AUC |
|-------|------|-------|------|-------|------|-------|------|
| 0 | **.890** | 8 | .756 | 16 | .814 | 24 | .831 |
| 1 | .777 | 9 | .760 | 17 | .836 | 25 | .817 |

**Key**: Layer 0 (embedding) is best — lexical/surface signal. All transformer layers WEAK (0.71-0.84). No "injection detection" layer.

### The Selectivity Problem (Key Finding)
- **Push direction** (Test 2, last_token): AUC 0.96 → CB knows HOW to push (comply → refuse)
- **Selectivity** (Test 3, last_token): AUC 0.83 → CB poorly knows WHEN to push (injection vs clean)
- **Consequence**: CB pushes ALL tool calls, not just harmful ones → benign destroyed
- **This is the paper's central finding**: the asymmetry between push and selectivity predicts the Pareto tradeoff
